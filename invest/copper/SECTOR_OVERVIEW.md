## Why Copper Now

Copper is in a structural deficit with multiple demand tailwinds:
- **JP Morgan projects 330,000 metric ton refined copper shortfall in 2026**
- ICSG revised from 209K ton surplus to 150K ton deficit — the market flipped
- Copper prices surged above $13,000/ton
- Declining ore grades + decade-long underinvestment in new mines
- Supply response takes 10–15 years (mine discovery to production)

## Demand Drivers

1. **AI data centers** — massive copper wiring for power distribution
2. **EV transition** — EVs use 3–4x more copper than ICE vehicles
3. **Grid upgrades** — $2T+ in global grid modernization planned
4. **Renewable energy** — solar/wind installations are copper-intensive
5. **General electrification** — the world is electrifying everything
