## The SpaceX IPO

### What's Happening
- **SpaceX is going public in 2026.** Elon Musk confirmed IPO reports are "accurate."
- **Target timing:** June 2026 or H2 2026
- **Valuation:** $1.25T (current private) → up to $1.5T at IPO
- **Raise:** $25–50B+ (would be the largest IPO in history, surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record)
- **xAI merged into SpaceX** on Feb 2, 2026 — combined entity goes public

### What You're Buying
SpaceX is three businesses in one:
1. **Starlink** — satellite internet (7,000+ satellites, $10B+ revenue in 2025, +60% YoY, already profitable, ~2/3 of SpaceX revenue)
2. **Launch services** — dominant rocket launch provider (Falcon 9, Starship)
3. **xAI** — Musk's AI company, now merged in (adds AI narrative to valuation)

### Why It Matters
- At $1.5T, SpaceX would instantly become one of the top 5 most valuable companies globally
- Starlink alone is a $10B+ revenue business growing 60% — comparable to early Netflix or AWS
- Space data centers (orbital compute) is the next frontier — solar-powered, zero cooling costs
- Retail investor access to SpaceX for the first time ever

## Starlink Competitive Landscape

| Company | Satellites | Status |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Starlink (SpaceX)** | 7,000+ | Dominant, profitable, $10B+ revenue |
| Amazon Kuiper | ~80 (launching) | Years behind, $10B+ committed |
| OneWeb (Eutelsat) | ~630 | Operational but smaller scale |
| Telesat Lightspeed | Planned | Still building |

Starlink has an insurmountable lead. The economics of satellite internet favor the first-mover at scale — more satellites = better coverage = more subscribers = more revenue = more satellites. Classic network effect.
