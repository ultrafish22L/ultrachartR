## Overview

Meta Platforms is the world's largest social media company (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads) and one of the most aggressive AI investors. Meta is deploying AI across its advertising platform (driving higher ad relevance and revenue), developing open-source Llama models, and building massive AI infrastructure. The company recently signed a $60B+ deal with AMD and a $3B deal with Nebius for AI data centers.

## Bull Case

- AI ad optimization is the most immediate, proven AI monetization story in the market
- $201B revenue with 22% growth — Meta is profitable while spending aggressively on AI
- Llama open-source strategy creates ecosystem influence and talent attraction
- Instagram Reels and Threads still growing, adding new monetizable surfaces
- Forward P/E of ~23x is attractive for a company growing 22% with AI optionality
- $60B AMD deal and diversified chip strategy reduce Nvidia dependency

## Bear Case

- 2026 expenses expected to grow "significantly faster" than revenue (CFO warning)
- Stock down ~18% from $796 high — market cautious on CapEx trajectory
- Reality Labs (metaverse) continues losing $15B+/year with uncertain payoff
- Regulatory risk in EU and US around data privacy and AI content
- Ad market sensitivity to macroeconomic conditions

## Key Risks

- CapEx escalation: 2026 expenses growing faster than revenue could compress margins
- Reality Labs losses remain a drag — investors patience wearing thin
- Regulatory crackdown on AI-generated content or data usage
- Competition from TikTok, YouTube, and emerging platforms
- Economic recession risk impacts advertising budgets

## Summary

Meta is the rare company where AI is already generating clear, measurable ROI through advertising optimization. At ~$654 with a forward P/E of ~23x, it's among the most reasonably valued mega-cap AI plays. The Llama ecosystem, massive infrastructure buildout, and diversified chip strategy position Meta well for the AI era. The main concern is expense growth outpacing revenue in 2026 as data center capacity comes online. But with $201B in revenue and strong profitability, Meta can afford to invest heavily.
