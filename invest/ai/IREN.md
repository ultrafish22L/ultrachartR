## Bull Case

- Massive Microsoft deal ($9.7B) validates the Bitcoin-to-AI pivot
- Strong cash position ($2.8B) provides runway without immediate dilution risk
- MSCI USA Index inclusion brings passive fund inflows
- Q1 FY26 showed record revenue and net income — proof of execution
- Renewable energy positioning appeals to ESG-focused investors
- $2.3B of $3.4B ARR target already contracted — strong visibility
- Lowest P/E of the three (46.7x) — relatively more reasonable valuation

## Bear Case

- Stock down 47% from $76.87 high
- Q2 FY26 EPS miss (-$0.52) raised execution concerns
- Bitcoin mining to AI/HPC pivot is still being proven at full scale
- Fiscal year structure (ending June) can confuse comparisons
- Inconsistent profitability — swings between record profits and EPS misses
- Revenue concentration risk with Microsoft as dominant customer

## Key Risks

- Pivot execution: transitioning from crypto mining to AI cloud is non-trivial
- Microsoft dependency — single customer represents outsized revenue share
- GPU supply chain risks for 140,000 unit delivery target
- Bitcoin mining revenue declining as focus shifts — transition period earnings volatility
- Competition from established data center operators (Equinix, Digital Realty) and AI-native players

## Summary

IREN is the smallest and most affordable of the three by valuation metrics, with a compelling pivot story from Bitcoin mining to AI infrastructure backed by a $9.7B Microsoft contract. The $2.8B cash position and MSCI inclusion are tangible positives. However, the pivot is still being proven, and the volatile earnings (record quarter followed by EPS miss) suggest the transition has rough patches. Of the three, IREN has the most diversified origin story and the strongest balance sheet relative to its size.
