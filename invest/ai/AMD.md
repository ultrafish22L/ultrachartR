## Overview

AMD is the primary competitor to Nvidia in AI accelerators and to Intel in CPUs. The company designs GPUs (Instinct series), data center CPUs (EPYC), and consumer processors. AMD recently secured a landmark $60B+ multi-year deal with Meta Platforms, its biggest validation as a credible alternative to Nvidia in AI.

## The Meta Deal

- **Structure:** Multi-year, multi-generation partnership for up to 6 gigawatts of AMD Instinct GPUs
- **Value:** Estimated $60B+ over 5 years (WSJ reports potentially $100B+)
- **Timeline:** Deployment begins H2 2026 with MI450 accelerators
- **Significance:** Largest single validation of AMD as a credible Nvidia alternative

## Bull Case

- Meta deal is transformational — $60B+ in committed revenue over 5 years
- AI market share climbing from 9% to 15%+ — significant growth runway
- MI450 architecture could close the performance gap with Nvidia
- 72% earnings growth expected in 2026, 60% in 2027
- Trading at fraction of Nvidia's valuation for similar growth trajectory
- CPU business (EPYC) continues taking share from Intel in data centers

## Bear Case

- Still distant #2 to Nvidia with only ~9% AI accelerator share
- Software ecosystem (ROCm) significantly behind Nvidia's CUDA
- Meta deal revenue doesn't start until H2 2026 — execution risk remains
- Custom silicon (Broadcom, Marvell) represents another competitive threat
- Consumer PC/gaming segment could drag during economic slowdown

## Key Risks

- Nvidia's response: Rubin architecture may widen performance gap again
- ROCm software maturity — customers value CUDA ecosystem
- Execution on MI450 ramp for Meta deal is critical
- Customer concentration: Meta deal is outsized portion of forward estimates
- Cyclical semiconductor risk

## Summary

AMD is the best-positioned challenger to Nvidia's AI dominance. The $60B+ Meta deal is a game-changer that validates AMD's AI strategy and provides massive revenue visibility. At ~$311B market cap vs Nvidia's $4.37T, AMD offers significantly more upside if it can execute on closing the performance gap. The risk is that Nvidia's ecosystem advantage and next-gen Rubin architecture maintain the status quo. The stock outperformed Nvidia in 2025 (+77% vs +39%), suggesting the market is starting to price in AMD's rising AI credibility.
