## Overview

TSMC is the world's largest dedicated contract chip manufacturer (foundry) with approximately 70% global market share. Every major AI chip — Nvidia's GPUs, AMD's accelerators, Broadcom's ASICs, Apple's processors — is manufactured at TSMC. The company is the single most critical node in the entire semiconductor supply chain.

## Why It's #3

- **Monopoly position:** 70% foundry market share — no viable alternative at leading edge
- **AI chip agnostic:** Wins regardless of whether Nvidia, AMD, Broadcom, or custom chips dominate
- **Technology leadership:** Most advanced process nodes (3nm, 2nm) years ahead of competitors
- **Revenue visibility:** Management bullish on 2026 with ~30% revenue growth guidance
- **AI revenue CAGR:** 60% compounded annual growth rate in AI-related revenue from 2024–2029

## Bull Case

- Every AI chip needs TSMC — pure picks-and-shovels play
- 60% AI revenue CAGR through 2029 provides multi-year growth runway
- Pricing power: advanced nodes command premium ASPs with limited competition
- Geographic diversification: building fabs in Arizona, Japan, Germany
- 2nm process ramp in 2025–2026 drives another upgrade cycle

## Bear Case

- Geopolitical risk: Taiwan Strait tensions are existential for the company
- Stock at ~$353, pricing in strong growth
- CapEx intensive — massive fab construction costs
- Samsung and Intel attempting to compete at advanced nodes (long-term risk)
- Cyclical exposure if AI spending plateaus

## Key Risks

- **Geopolitical risk is the #1 concern** — any Taiwan conflict would disrupt global chip supply
- Natural disaster risk (earthquakes in Taiwan)
- Customer concentration: Nvidia + Apple represent large revenue share
- Technology risk if EUV lithography hits physical limits

## Summary

TSMC is the most strategically important company in AI — possibly in the entire global economy. With 70% foundry share and years of technology lead, it manufactures virtually every cutting-edge AI chip. The 60% AI revenue CAGR through 2029 makes the growth story compelling, but the geopolitical overhang around Taiwan is the elephant in the room. For investors who can stomach that risk, TSMC is as close to an AI tollbooth as exists.
