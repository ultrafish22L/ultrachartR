## Overview

Nebius Group is a full-stack AI infrastructure company spun out of Yandex. It provides GPU cloud clusters, storage, and AI platform services. The company has secured major multi-year contracts with Microsoft ($17.4B) and Meta ($3B), positioning it as a key AI infrastructure supplier to hyperscalers.

## Bull Case

- Explosive revenue growth trajectory (351% YoY in 2025)
- Anchor contracts with two of the largest tech companies (Microsoft + Meta = $20.4B)
- Revenue acceleration: Q4 grew 547% YoY, showing momentum
- Full-stack offering (compute + storage + platform) provides stickiness
- 2.5 GW capacity target signals massive scale ambitions

## Bear Case

- Still unprofitable — Q4 EPS miss (-$0.68 vs -$0.54 expected)
- P/E of 226x is extremely stretched even for a growth stock
- Scaling from $530M annual revenue to $7–9B ARR in one year is an enormous leap
- Stock down 37% from highs amid AI sentiment cooling
- Yandex heritage may create geopolitical perception risks
- Execution risk on infrastructure buildout at this pace

## Key Risks

- Customer concentration: Microsoft and Meta represent outsized share of backlog
- Capital intensity of building out to 2.5 GW capacity
- Competition from established cloud providers and other AI infra startups
- Revenue recognition timing — ARR targets vs actual recognized revenue
- Geopolitical risks related to company origins

## Summary

Nebius has the most aggressive growth ramp of the three stocks, targeting a ~15x revenue increase from $530M to $7–9B ARR in a single year. The Microsoft and Meta contracts provide a strong foundation, but the execution challenge is immense. The stock offers significant analyst-implied upside (~70%), but carries high valuation risk at 226x earnings while still unprofitable.
