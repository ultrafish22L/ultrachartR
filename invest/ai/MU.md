## Overview

Micron Technology is the leading U.S. memory chip manufacturer, producing DRAM, NAND flash, and critically, High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) — the specialized memory that sits directly on AI accelerator packages. HBM is essential for AI training and inference, and Micron's HBM3E products are in extreme demand. The company's entire 2026 HBM production is already sold out.

- Average analyst target ($349) is below current price (~$380) — stock may be ahead of itself

## Key Risks

- **Cyclicality is the #1 risk** — memory booms always eventually bust
- Competition: Samsung and SK Hynix ramping HBM capacity aggressively
- Stock trading above average analyst target (~$349 vs ~$380) — contrarian signal
- AI demand concentration: HBM revenue depends on continued GPU shipment growth
- Technology transitions (HBM3E → HBM4) involve execution risk

## Summary

Micron is experiencing the strongest demand environment in memory industry history, driven by AI's insatiable appetite for high-bandwidth memory. With entire 2026 production sold out, 278% earnings growth expected, and the HBM market projected to hit $100B by 2028, the fundamentals are extraordinary. However, the ~301% stock run and the fact that the current price exceeds the average analyst target suggest caution — memory cycles have historically been brutal, and the stock may be pricing in peak conditions. For believers in a sustained AI memory supercycle, Micron offers direct exposure to one of AI's most critical components.
