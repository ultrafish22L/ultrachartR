## Overview

Nvidia is the world's most valuable company and the backbone of the AI revolution. Its GPUs are the industry standard for training and running large language models, and it commands over 80% market share in AI accelerators. The company designs and sells data center GPUs (H100, Blackwell, upcoming Rubin), gaming GPUs, and networking solutions.

## Why It's #1

- **Market dominance:** 80%+ share of AI accelerator market
- **Revenue scale:** $216B in FY2026 — no other pure AI play comes close
- **Growth persistence:** 65% YoY revenue growth at massive scale
- **Ecosystem lock-in:** CUDA software platform creates deep moat
- **Forward visibility:** $500B+ in revenue commitments for Blackwell and Rubin systems through end of 2026
- **Every AI company depends on Nvidia:** OpenAI, Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon all major customers

## Bull Case

- Demand continues to outstrip supply for Blackwell GPUs
- Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78B implies continued acceleration
- Rubin (next-gen) architecture launching creates another upgrade cycle
- Data center revenue is the dominant segment and still growing rapidly
- AI inference demand scaling alongside training demand

## Bear Case

- $4.36T valuation prices in enormous continued growth
- Customer concentration risk — hyperscalers could shift to custom silicon (AMD, Marvell, Broadcom ASICs)
- AMD's Meta deal signals real competitive pressure emerging
- Geopolitical risk: China export restrictions limit TAM
- Any slowdown in AI CapEx spending could hit hard at this valuation

## Key Risks

- Valuation compression if growth decelerates even slightly
- Custom silicon competition from Broadcom (Google TPU), Marvell (AWS/MSFT), AMD (Meta)
- Cyclicality — semiconductor demand can shift rapidly
- Regulatory/geopolitical restrictions on chip exports

## Summary

Nvidia is the foundational AI investment — the company that makes the picks and shovels for the entire AI gold rush. At $4.36T it's priced for perfection, but it keeps delivering: $216B revenue, 65% growth, and $78B guidance for next quarter. The risk is that at this scale, the law of large numbers eventually catches up, and custom silicon alternatives chip away at market share. But for now, Nvidia remains the single most important company in AI.
