## Overview

Broadcom is a diversified semiconductor and infrastructure software company that has emerged as a critical AI player through its custom AI chip (ASIC) business and networking solutions. The company designs custom AI accelerators for hyperscalers (most notably Google's TPU), provides high-speed networking chips for data centers, and owns VMware for enterprise software.

## Bull Case

- Custom AI chips are a viable, cheaper alternative to Nvidia GPUs for specific workloads
- Networking revenue scales with every GPU deployed — agnostic to chip vendor
- VMware integration adds $12B+ in recurring software revenue
- 34% analyst-implied upside to $420
- AI custom silicon TAM expanding as more hyperscalers design custom chips

## Bear Case

- $1.5T+ valuation is already substantial
- Custom ASIC business is lumpy — dependent on design wins with a handful of hyperscalers
- Competition from Marvell in custom silicon space
- VMware integration risks and customer pricing pushback
- Revenue growth (24%) slower than pure-play AI peers

## Key Risks

- Customer concentration in custom silicon (Google is largest)
- Marvell winning AWS and Microsoft custom chip designs
- Nvidia's networking push (Spectrum-X) threatens Broadcom's networking dominance
- VMware enterprise churn from aggressive pricing changes

## Summary

Broadcom is the best-positioned company to benefit from AI regardless of which chip architecture wins. Its networking chips go into every data center, and its custom silicon business offers an alternative path to Nvidia GPUs. The 292% earnings growth and diversified revenue base make it arguably the most well-rounded AI investment. The March 4 earnings report is a near-term catalyst.
