## Investment Thesis by Layer

### Tier 1: Chip Makers (NVDA, AVGO, TSM, AMD, MRVL, MU)
These companies build the silicon that powers AI. They benefit regardless of which AI models or applications win — as long as the buildout continues. This is the "picks and shovels" tier.

- **NVDA** is the dominant player (80%+ market share) but at $4.37T is priced for perfection
- **AMD** ($191) offers the best risk/reward as the rising challenger with a $60B+ Meta deal
- **AVGO** is the safest diversified play — custom chips + networking + VMware software
- **TSM** is the indispensable foundry — makes everyone's chips — but carries geopolitical risk
- **MRVL** ($78) is the smallest and most asymmetric bet — custom silicon for AWS, MSFT, Google
- **MU** is the HBM memory play — sold out through 2026, but may be priced beyond fundamentals

### Tier 2: AI Platform & Cloud (MSFT, GOOGL, META)
These companies are both the biggest AI spenders ($500B+ combined 2026 CapEx) and the biggest AI beneficiaries through their platforms.

- **MSFT** has the best AI monetization path (400M+ Office users + Azure + OpenAI) at a reasonable 25x PE
- **GOOGL** has the deepest AI research (DeepMind) and fastest cloud growth (48% YoY) at 28x PE
- **META** is the only company where AI is already directly driving revenue via ad optimization, at 28x PE

### Tier 3: Infrastructure (VRT)
The picks-and-shovels play on the physical layer — every GPU needs power and cooling.

- **VRT** is the pure-play on data center buildout with a $15B doubled backlog, but at 72x PE the market has caught on

## Key Themes & Observations

### 1. The CapEx Arms Race Is Staggering
- Google: $175–185B in 2026 CapEx (2x 2025)
- Microsoft: ~$150B annualized
- Meta: "significantly faster" expense growth in 2026
- CoreWeave: $30–35B
- Micron: $20B
- **Total hyperscaler CapEx in 2026 likely exceeds $500B**

### 2. Valuation Spectrum Is Wide
- Best value: **MSFT** (25x PE), **MRVL** (27x PE), **META** (28x PE), **GOOGL** (28x PE)
- Expensive but growing: **NVDA** (37x PE), **MU** (36x PE)
- Premium: **AVGO** (66x PE), **VRT** (72x PE), **AMD** (72x PE)
- Richest: **TSM** (167x PE)

### 3. The Nvidia Alternatives Are Real
AMD's $60B+ Meta deal, Broadcom's Google TPU dominance, and Marvell's wins at AWS/MSFT/Google signal real diversification away from Nvidia. This is healthy for the ecosystem but a long-term risk for NVDA's market share.

### 4. Memory Is the Bottleneck
Micron's entire 2026 HBM production is sold out. HBM TAM is projected to grow from $35B (2025) to $100B (2028). This is one of the tightest supply/demand dynamics in the AI chain.

### 5. AI Revenue Is Becoming Real
- Microsoft Azure AI: $24B ARR
- Google Cloud: +48% growth
- Meta: AI ad optimization directly boosting revenue
- Nvidia: $216B in AI-driven revenue
- This is no longer speculative — AI is generating hundreds of billions in measurable revenue

## Risk Factors (Macro)

1. **AI CapEx pullback** — if hyperscalers slow spending, the entire chain suffers
2. **Interest rates** — higher rates pressure capex-heavy, debt-funded companies
3. **Geopolitical** — Taiwan (TSMC), China export controls (NVDA), trade tensions
4. **AI regulation** — emerging EU/US frameworks could constrain AI deployment
5. **Bubble risk** — $500B+ in annual CapEx assumes AI demand grows indefinitely
6. **Concentration risk** — a handful of hyperscalers drive most of the spending
