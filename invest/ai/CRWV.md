## Overview

CoreWeave is a purpose-built GPU cloud platform for large-scale AI infrastructure, training, and inference. Founded in 2017, it IPO'd on Nasdaq in March 2025. The company positions itself as "The Essential Cloud for AI."

## Bull Case

- Massive $66.8B contracted revenue backlog provides strong visibility
- Hyper-growth: 168% YoY in 2025, guiding 140%+ growth in 2026
- First-mover advantage in purpose-built AI cloud infrastructure
- All new capacity contracts expected to generate revenue by end of 2026
- Declining interest rates on debt (300bps reduction in weighted avg rate)

## Bear Case

- Stock down ~58% from $187 all-time high — significant momentum loss
- Enormous CapEx burn: $30–35B in 2026 alone (2x the $14.9B spent in 2025)
- Heavy debt load from $18B+ in capital raises
- Q1 2026 guidance of only $0–40M adjusted operating income on ~$2B revenue — thin margins
- AI bubble concerns weighed on stock after Q4 earnings report
- Profitability path unclear given massive infrastructure investment cycle

## Key Risks

- Execution risk on scaling infrastructure to meet $12–13B revenue target
- Customer concentration risk (backlog details not fully public)
- Interest rate sensitivity given high leverage
- AI demand could plateau if enterprise adoption slows
- Competition from hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) building similar GPU capacity

## Summary

CoreWeave is a high-growth, high-burn AI infrastructure play with the largest revenue base among pure-play AI cloud providers. The contracted backlog is impressive, but the stock's steep decline from highs reflects market skepticism about the sustainability of AI infrastructure spending at this pace. The company is essentially betting that AI compute demand will continue to outstrip supply for years.
