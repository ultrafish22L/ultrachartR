## Key Risk #1: Dollar Devaluation

### What's Happening
- DXY fell below 97 — 4-year low
- Morgan Stanley forecasts DXY dropping to 94 by Q2 2026
- Consensus: 3–5% annual decline, could accelerate
- Fed cutting rates toward 3.0–3.25% (Goldman) or 3.4% (CBO) by Q4

### Why It's Happening
1. **Fed rate cuts** — lower rates = lower dollar demand
2. **Fiscal deterioration** — $1.9T deficit, debt at 101% GDP heading to 120%
3. **"Big Beautiful Bill"** — expanded fiscal spending adding to debt burden
4. **Tariff uncertainty** — 15% universal tariff (Feb 24) creating policy chaos
5. **De-dollarization** — central banks slowly diversifying reserves away from USD
6. **Interest costs** — $270B in Q1 alone (2nd largest budget item after Social Security)

### What It Means for Investments
- **International stocks benefit** — foreign earnings translate to more dollars (VXUS +32% past year)
- **Commodities rise** — priced in dollars, cheaper for foreign buyers
- **Gold surging** — JP Morgan targets $5,055–$6,300/oz by end 2026
- **US exporters benefit** — products cheaper abroad
- **US importers hurt** — costs rise, margin pressure
- **Inflation risk** — weaker dollar = imported inflation

## Key Risk #2: Sudden Market Drop

### Current Warning Signals
1. **Buffett Indicator at 219%** — highest ever (preceded 2000, 2008 crashes)
2. **79% of institutions expect a correction in 2026** (Natixis survey, $30T AUM)
3. **58% probability** of S&P dropping to 6,200 or below (prediction markets)
4. **8% put-implied probability** of a 30%+ crash
5. **Top 10 stocks = 40% of market cap** — extreme concentration
6. **Insider selling picking up** — smart money reducing exposure
7. **Consumer sentiment at historic lows** — demand could crack
8. **Job growth revised sharply down** — 2025 net jobs only 181K
9. **Geopolitical escalation** — Iran strikes (March 2) spiked VIX

### Potential Catalysts for a Drop
- Tariff escalation beyond 15% universal rate
- Inflation re-acceleration above 4% (some analysts warn of this)
- Fed forced to pause/reverse rate cuts
- AI CapEx slowdown or disappointment
- Geopolitical escalation (Iran, Taiwan, trade war)
- Credit event or corporate debt stress
- Consumer spending collapse

### Historical Context
- Corrections (10-20% drops) are normal and healthy — happen every ~2 years
- Crashes (30%+) are rare — roughly once per decade
- Current setup rhymes with late 1999/early 2000 (extreme concentration, high valuations, new technology euphoria)

## Key Risk #3: Tariffs & Trade War

### Current Situation
- **15% universal global tariff** imposed Feb 24 under Section 122
- Supreme Court temporarily challenged trade authority (Feb 20)
- If maintained for full 150-day limit, could significantly cool economy by late 2026
- **$1,500/household** average tax increase from tariffs
- Consumers bear 67% of tariff costs by July 2026
- Tariffs adding ~0.5% to core PCE inflation

### Investment Implications
- Small caps / domestic companies outperforming (Russell 2000 beating Nasdaq by 9% over 30 days)
- Multinational exporters facing margin pressure
- Import-dependent companies at risk
- Reshoring beneficiaries: industrials, construction, domestic manufacturing

- Companies with high USD-denominated debt
- Long-duration growth stocks (if rates rise)
